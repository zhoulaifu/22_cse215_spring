Exercise 1 (10 points)

Suppose a1 , a2 , a3 , . . . is a sequence defined as follows:

a1 = 1, a2 = 3, and ak = ak−2 + 2ak−1 for all integers k ≥ 3.

Prove that an is odd for all integers n ≥ 1.

Exercise 2 (10 points)

Suppose that g1, g2, g3, . . . is a sequence defined as follows:

g1 = 3, g2 = 5, and gk = 3gk−1 − 2gk−2 for all integers k ≥ 3.

Prove that gn = 2n + 1 forall integers n ≥ 1.

Exercise 3 (15 points)

The triangle inequality states that for all real numbers a and b, |a + b| ≤ |a| + |b|. Use the the triangle inequality and mathematical induction to prove:

For any n real numbers a1, a2, …, and an,

\[|a_1 + a_2 + ... + a_n| \leq |a_1| + |a_2| + ... + |a_n|\]

Exercise 4 (25 = 5 + 10 + 10 points)

Let f be a sequence defined recursively as follows.

f1 = 1, and fk = fk−1 +2k for all integers k ≥ 2

  1. Write out fk for k = 1, 2,…, 5.
  2. Derive an explicit form of the sequence.
  3. Prove that your explcit form corresponds to the original recursive definition. [Hint: To do so, you can name your explcit form sequence as Fk, and prove: for all k ≥ 1, Fk = fk.]

Exercise 5 (20 points)

A certain computer program executes twice as many operations when it runs with an input of size k as when it runs with an input of size k − 1 (where k is an integer and k > 1). When the program runs with an input of size 1, it executes seven operations. How many operations does it execute when it runs with an input of size 25?

Exercise 6 (20 points)

Consider the two recursively defined sequences.

  1. a1 = 0 and ak = 2ak−1 + k − 1 for all integers k ≥ 2

  2. a1 = 0 and ak = (ak−1 + 1)2 for all integers k ≥ 2

    Determine whether the two recursively defined sequences satisfies the explicit formula an = (n − 1)2, for all integers n ≥ 1.
